Purpose

Reference data is needed to produce and validate our products. Within our project we carefully track which data has been used for training and which data has been used for validation.

A key issue is collecting a sufficient amount of reference data across many different landscapes and crop types. The map below shows the current state of reference data on a country basis.

Sources

The project is attempting to collect reference data from a variety of sources. Useful data on a global scale is very limited and some creative ideas are required. Some of our current sources are below.

  • Custom Mobile Application
  • Past Field Visits
  • Derived from Very High Resolution Imagery
  • Secondary Sources from Projects or Research
  • Statistical Data for Area Comparisons

Ground Data Form

This standard ground data form is designed and developed by Dr. Russell G. Congalton. This ground data form allows you to collect standardized ground data (or field data) samples from 90 m by 90 m (3 by 3 Landsat pixels) area (or 0.27 m2 area). Data collected includes, GPS location, land cover types, and digital photographs.

Ideally, sample locations are identified based on stratified random sampling (stratified by road network, and randomized by locating points every few minutes of drive or certain distance travelled).
