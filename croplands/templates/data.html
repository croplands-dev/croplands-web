Training and Validation Data

Reference data is needed to produce and validate our projects. Within our project we carefully track which data has been used for training and which data has been used for validation, although as you explore the data on this site you will not be able to distinguish between the datasets.

 

Issues and Challenges

Getting useful data can be difficult. Two of the primary issues that we run into are mixed pixels and positional error.

The first issue, mixed pixels is due to satellite images not providing a high enough resolution. For example, landsat images have pixels approximately 30 by 30 meters in size. Within this pixel there may be multiple land cover types as seen in the diagram below. To solve this issue, we try to limit our data to pure pixels, areas that are homogeneous across an area equal to 3 by 3 landsat pixels.

The second issue requires us to make use of secondary data such as photos to determine if the location is accurate. For example, many of the data locations are along the edge of the areas of interest due to the limitations of data collection.

 

Ground Data Form

This standard ground data form is designed and developed by Dr. Russell G. Congalton. This ground data form allows you to collect standardized ground data (or field data) samples from 90 m by 90 m (3 by 3 Landsat pixels) area (or 0.27 m2 area). Data collected includes, GPS location, land cover types, and digital photographs. Ideally, sample locations are identified based on stratified random sampling (stratified by road network, and randomized by locating points every few minutes of drive or certain distance travelled).

The form can be downloaded here.

 

Sources of Data

The project is attempting to collect reference data from a variety of sources. Useful data on a global scale is very limited and some creative ideas are required. Some of our current sources are below.

  • Mobile Application
  • Field Visits
  • Google Street View
  • Very High Resolution Imagery (VHRI)
  • Secondary Sources from Projects/Research (e.g., LUCAS, USDA CDL, published papers, published maps)
  • Statistical Data for Area Comparisons (e.g., MIRCA2000, FAO)
